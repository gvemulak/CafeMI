Classes Offered

CafeMI - Brewing Excellence is a weekend enrichment program that offers the following classes for middle school and high school students

Math

  • Math Level 1 - Creative Problem Solving (5th Grade)
  • Math Level 2 - Elementary Algebra combined with basic Geometry
  • Math Level 3 - Intermediate Algebra and Geometry
  • Math Level 4 - Advanced Algebra and Introduction to Pre-Calculus
  • Math Level 5 - Pre-Calculus, Probability and test preparation

    • College Prep

  • Prepare for either the SAT or ACT through in-class instruction and practice for Reading and Math
  • The class is open only to High school students in grades 9 and above

    • Public Speaking

  • Public Speaking - Junior Level (5th and 6th grades)
  • Public Speaking - Senior Level (7th grade and above)
  • Selected students will get the opportunity to represent Brewing Excellence at local MIFA tournaments in the metro Detroit area.

    • Crack the Code

  • Fall semester will cover principles of developing a website and an introduction to Generative AI
  • The Spring semester will cover the Python programming language
  • This is an online class offered on Sunday mornings and will include a final team project

    • Additional Opportunities

  • MOEM Testing
  • AMC 8/10 Testing
  • Pascal/Gauss Testing
  • End of semester competitions for Speech, Debate, and Impromptu on stage.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    See below for answers to some frequently asked questions. You can also send an email to info@brewingexcellence.org in case of additional clarifications or reach out to the staff.

    1. Is there a recommended level my child should start with for Math?

    Based on our experience students in grade 5 should register for Math Level 1 and the levels advance by grade all the way to Math Level 5 which is recommended for students in grade 9. Under rare circumstances you can have your child test out of a level and advance to the next level after consulting with the teacher at the beginning of the Fall semester. Students can also take a lower level to solidify the concepts in a particular topic.

    2. Will there be makeup classes if my student misses a class?

    We do not offer makeup classes at this time. The students are responsible to check the Google Classroom and complete homework for the class that they miss. The only exception is the Math Olypiad exams. We will occasionally conduct makeup tests within a week of the MOEMS test date usually at the Canton library.

    3. What is the curriculum for the classes?

    Please check the Classes section for the topics covered in the class. You can reach out to the subject teacher in case you need further details

    4. What are the competitive contests that my child can attend through CafeMI?

    CafeMI-Brewing Excellence offers a number of opportunities to excel throughout the academic year that are included as part of the Math and Public Speaking class payments.

  • Nov - Mar (5 MOEM tests) - Math Olympiad Levels E(Elementary) and M(Middle school)
  • Nov - American Mathematics Competitions AMC10B
  • Jan - MathCounts Invitational
  • Jan - American Mathematics Competitions AMC8
  • Jan - Mid-year Public Speaking Impromptu, Debate and Speech competition
  • Feb - Pascal contest conducted by the University of Waterloo for 9th graders and below
  • May - MIFA State Middle Level Forensics Contest
  • May - Gauss contest conducted by the University of Waterloo for 7th and 8th graders
  • June - Annual Public Speaking Impromptu, Debate and Speech competition.
    • Please refer to the Annual calendar for the full list with actual dates

    5. How are students graded for end of the year academic honor roll awards?

    Each teacher has grading criteria that they use to determine the top three students in the class that are recognized with an honor roll trophy at the end of the year. Typically the rankings are based on a weighted score that accounts for class attendanece, homework submissions, and performance on class tests.
